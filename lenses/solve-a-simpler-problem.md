---
name: solve-a-simpler-problem
when: The problem is too big/general to grapple with; you don't know where to start.
when_not: Simplifying strips out the essence (the core difficulty lies exactly in what you're cutting).
---
Solve a deliberately trimmed version: fewer cases, one dimension, a concrete example instead of the general one. What's the simplest version of this problem that's still non-trivial — and what does solving it teach about the full one?
