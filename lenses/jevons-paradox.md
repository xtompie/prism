---
name: jevons-paradox
when: you're increasing efficiency or lowering the cost of using a resource (time, money, energy, attention)
when_not: demand is fixed and won't grow from easier or cheaper access
---
When something becomes cheaper or easier, total usage often rises and eats the savings — the improvement drives more use, not less. Ask: "won't the per-unit savings vanish because we simply start doing much more of it?"
