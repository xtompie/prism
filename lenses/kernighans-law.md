---
name: kernighans-law
when: you're tempted by the cleverest, most ingenious solution at the edge of your ability
when_not: the problem is simple and the solution is obvious and easy to trace
---
Debugging and maintenance are harder than writing, so if you build at the peak of your cleverness you'll have no headroom left to fix it later. Leave a margin of simplicity and ask: "will I understand and fix this in six months when it breaks?"
