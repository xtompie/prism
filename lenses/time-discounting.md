---
name: time-discounting
when: A choice between a benefit now and a larger one later (debt, health, quality vs deadline).
when_not: The effects are immediate, or the future is genuinely unpredictable/worthless.
---
By reflex we value "now" disproportionately over "later," and the further out it is, the more we undervalue it — hence systematically plundering the future for a small relief today. Am I discounting the future sensibly, or just because it's distant and out of sight?
